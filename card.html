Toucans live in a rainforest canopies!

  • Scientific Name: Ramphastidae
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Lifespan: 20 yrs.
  • Length: 11.4 - 24.8 cm

"Toucan" is the common name given to numerous species of tropical American forest birds known for their large and strikingly colored bills. Toucans are monomorphic, which means that the male and female birds look the same as each other. The Toco Toucan is the largest toucan of the species, and it measures a huge 25 inches, with a beak that is 19 cm long and neon orange in color. Toucans are among the noisiest of forest birds; their repertoire includes loud barks, bugling calls, and harsh croaks.
